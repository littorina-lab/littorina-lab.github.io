Morphometric analysis of the copulative organ of mollusks of the saxatilis species complex (Coenogastropoda, Littorinidae): problems of identification and status of species

Granovitch A, Loskutova Z, Gracheva Y, Mikhailova N
January 2008

Abstract

Using the morphometric analysis and amplification of DNA with specific primers, we studied the male copulative organ of similar species of littorines from the “saxatilis” complex (Littorina saxatilis, L. arcana, L. compressa) in populations of the White and Barents Seas. Species-specific structural features of the L. compressa penis have been confirmed. No reliable interspecies differences in males L. saxatilis and L. arcana have been found previously. Females of these species differ in the structure of the pallial part of the oviduct. Females of L. arcana lay egg masses, females of L. saxatilis are ovoviviparous. The DNA fragment species-specific for L. arcana was used as a species marker to search for differences in the morphometric characteristics of the males of the two species. In general, data from the sympatric settlement L. arcana and L. saxatilis from the Barents Sea indicate the absence of diagnostic characters for males. At the same time, there is trend towards a more prominent manifestation of the trait “retraction of the penile filament” in males of L. arcana (“PCR +” mollusks amplifying a DNA marker). In addition, a larger proportion of males in the sympatric settlement of L. arcana and L. saxatilis is characterized by a multi-row arrangement of the penile glands. The absence of diagnostic characters that are rigidly connected with the molecular marker in the structure of the copulative apparatus of L. arcana and L. saxatilis males is explained by the phylogenetic proximity of sibling species and possible interspecific hybridization in natural settlements. This is also confirmed by the amplification of the DNA marker in females, the species of which can be independently identified by morpho-anatomical characters.
