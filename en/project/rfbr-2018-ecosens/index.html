Environmental monitoring of Arctic coastal ecosystems: Sensitivity to petroleum pollution (Arctic EcoSens)

RFBR #18-54-20001

The state of Arctic biocenoses reflects global dynamic processes associated with climate change and, at the same time, reflects local anthropogenic impact. In the Arctic, the rapid expansion of human economic activity such as oil and gas production is one of significant factors, which impact on natural communities requires a thorough assessment. In addition to practical significance, this situation also provides an opportunity to model biota dynamics in changing environmental conditions. In this regard, the main fundamental component of the project is the assessment of the degree of stability of Arctic biocenoses, the analysis of the mechanisms that ensure it in the gradient of environmental conditions, and development of new approaches for monitoring of Arctic biocenoses.

To fullfill the project's goals, it is important that the structure of the complex “pollution effect” can be quantified as the ratio of the components of polyaromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) of biogenic, petrogenic and pyrogenic origin (a “fingerprint” passport of PAH pollution at each site studied), and measuring the concentration of heavy metals (working package (WP) 1). This makes it possible to assess the correlation of the “pollution effect” with the structural biocenotic parameters of the model intertidal communities of salt marches and rocky intertidal sites (WP). Moreover, the use of experimental methods such as proteomics and metabolics to assess modell communities allows us to draw conclusions about the effect of factors and the state of biota (WP 3). The main objective of the project is to assess the impact of factors associated with oil and gas production and associated anthropogenic activity in the polar region on model biocenoses using modern geochemical, synecological and autecological approaches, modernization of the methodology for monitoring biocenoses of the Barents Sea at oil production sites, and exchange of experience between Russian and Norwegian research institutes and universities.

The project uses the strong side of decades-long scientific cooperation between Russia and Norway, which involves environmental monitoring of the Barents Sea, development of environmental impact standards for both countries.
